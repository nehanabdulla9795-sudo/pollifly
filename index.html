Our Mission

What happens to our food
when the bees are gone?

Pollifly was born from a simple but urgent question. Our mission is to build intelligent, deployable technology that compensates for the loss of natural pollinators and puts the power of reliable crop pollination back into the hands of farmers.

We believe that the future of agriculture must be resilient, autonomous, and deeply connected to the rhythms of nature. Pollifly is our first step toward that future — small in size, but significant in purpose.

The Vision

A world where technology and nature work in harmony to ensure food security for all

When the bees disappear,
our food follows.

Over 75% of the world's flowering plant species depend on pollinators. As bee populations collapse under the weight of pesticides, habitat loss and climate change, the crops we depend on are left without their most essential partner.

Unit P-01 · Prototype

Meet Pollifly

Precision-engineered for the garden of tomorrow

Pollifly is a miniature, quadrotor-inspired drone weighing under 50 grams. It navigates autonomously through crop rows using onboard computer vision, identifies flowering plants, and delivers targeted vibration pollination — all without human intervention.

How It Works

The Pollination
Process

From deployment to mission completion, here's how Pollifly autonomously restores crop pollination.

1

Field Deployment

The Pollifly drone is deployed at the field or greenhouse in the early morning, launched from a solar-powered base station with a pre-planned GPS route.

2

Autonomous Navigation

Using optical flow sensor for horizontal stability and ToF sensors for altitude, Pollifly glides through rows at a controlled height of 20–30 cm above the canopy.

3

Flower Detection

The onboard OV2640 camera streams live video to a lightweight computer vision model that identifies open female flowers in real time.

4

Vibration Pollination

Upon confirming a female flower target, the drone hovers precisely and activates a motorized brush that vibrates to mimic natural bee buzz pollination.

5

Mission Logging

Flight data, pollination events, and success metrics are transmitted wirelessly to the base station and synced to the cloud dashboard for farmer review.
